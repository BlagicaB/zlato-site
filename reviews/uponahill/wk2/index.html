Ever wonder how a first-year event lands a lineup this good? Artistic direction by Erik Hall.

Meet your opening-night headliner: Elizabeth Moen. September 2.

A Chicago-based songwriter originally from small-town Iowa, Moen combines indie-rock grit with the warmth of Midwestern folk - candid lyrics, powerful vocals and heartfelt storytelling. Her upcoming album Fields on Fire, produced by Spencer Tweedy, captures the beauty of life's uncertain moments with honesty and depth.

Put on one of her songs this week, then grab your tickets for September 2. It is going to be a special night on the hill.

Tickets at uponahill.org.

4 · Liam Kazar (Sept 30)

Visual: Liam Kazar press photo. IG: invite him as a Collaborator. Drop a song link in the first comment.

Closing the series on September 30: Liam Kazar.

Chicago-born and Brooklyn-based, Kazar blends folk-rock, pop, jazz and alt-country into a sound all his own. After years touring and collaborating with artists including Jeff Tweedy and Sam Evian, he released his acclaimed debut Due North. His latest album, Pilot Light, is intimate songwriting at its best - rich melodies and an honest look at love, loss and personal growth.

A perfect note to end year one on. Tickets for September 30 at uponahill.org.
